About

Revolutionising the relationship between Fashion and Fundraising

Ted Gladdish, who passed away in 2014, spent all of his working career in the fashion logistics and children's hospice sectors, turning specifically Demelza House into one of the most successful and forward looking children's hospice charity's, he also successfully led Noah's Ark children's hospice into a Â£10m capital appeal to build a new hospice in north London.

Glynn Manson, who passed away in 2015, was a founding member of Fenn Wright & Manson and at the forefront of new ways of thinking within the fashion sector.

In memory of Ted and Glynn, New Vision Foundation plan to work with the fashion industry in creating an ongoing legacy supporting children and young people.

Patrick Hollwey

Patrick Hollwey spent most of his working career in the mid market fashion sector as a director of Fenn Wright & Manson, also being a director and trustee of various hospice and youth charities including Demelza House and Noah's Ark. He is co-founder of Tennis For Free and involved with the Eric Evans trust lobbying to make grassroots sport high on government agenda.

Mat Gladdish

Mat Gladdish, son of Ted, had a background in International Senior Level Executive Search, before joining New Vision Funding, a consultancy set up by Ted and Patrick to advise Charities on their income generation strategies. Mat has a particular focus on fundraising and retail operations for Charities.

How will New Vision Foundation be structured?

A board of trustees representing the fashion, legal, financial and charitable sectors will be formed. All funds raised by the foundation will be dispersed across a range of causes on a quarterly basis as decided by the trustees. The management of the foundation and all operational procedures will be run by New Vision Funding Retail Ltd, a company owned by Patrick Hollwey and Mat Gladdish.

Which charity's will be supported?

New Vision Foundation will be mainly focussing on providing for Children's hospices and youth charities.
